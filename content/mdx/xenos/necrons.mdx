## Before the living galaxy

The Necrons began as the Necrontyr, a short-lived species embittered by harsh stars and the apparent immortality of the Old Ones. Their wars against the Old Ones led them to the C'tan, star-born entities that offered transformation through biotransference.

The promise was a trap. Necrontyr bodies became living metal while their souls were consumed. The new Necrons gained endurance and terrible weapons but lost much of what had made them a people.

## The War in Heaven

With the C'tan, the Necrons fought the Old Ones and the species created to resist them. The conflict devastated the galaxy and helped make the Warp more turbulent. At the war's end, the Necrons turned against the C'tan, shattering them into enslaved fragments.

They then entered the Great Sleep, retreating into tomb worlds to wait while younger species rose and fell.

## The awakening

Tomb worlds now stir across the galaxy. Some awaken intact beneath calculating rulers. Others suffer madness, decay, or corrupted protocols. The returned Silent King and the expansion of null-field technologies make the Necrons one of the defining powers of the Era Indomitus.
