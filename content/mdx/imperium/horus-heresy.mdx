## The promise before the betrayal

The Great Crusade reunited scattered human worlds beneath the Emperor and his genetically engineered sons, the Primarchs. Its victories were immense, but its foundations were brittle. Rivalries divided the Primarchs. The Imperial Truth denied the supernatural even as the Warp shaped the campaign. Compliance often meant conquest.

At Ullanor, the Emperor appointed Horus Lupercal as Warmaster and returned to Terra. Horus became the commander of the Crusade and the focus of pressures that would be turned against him.

## The fall of the Warmaster

Wounded on Davin by a weapon steeped in Chaos, Horus was shown visions crafted to exploit his pride, fear, and resentment. He accepted the powers behind those visions and began converting brother Primarchs, Legions, and Imperial institutions to rebellion.

The massacre at Isstvan III purged loyalists from traitor Legions. The Drop Site Massacre at Isstvan V shattered several loyalist Legions and revealed the scale of the conspiracy.

<Callout title="Why the Heresy succeeded">
The rebellion was not one betrayal but thousands: personal grudges, ideological conflicts, hidden cults, political ambitions, and the careful manipulation of the Warp.
</Callout>

## A war across the species

The Heresy engulfed the Imperium. Worlds changed allegiance. Titan Legions fought in burning cities. The Mechanicum fractured. The Ruinstorm disrupted loyalist movement and communication. Each Primarch brought unique virtues and failures into the conflict, magnifying both through armies built in their image.

The war culminated in the Siege of Terra. Horus's forces breached the outer defenses and assaulted the Imperial Palace. In the final confrontation aboard the Warmaster's flagship, the Emperor killed Horus but suffered mortal wounds.

<LoreQuote>
The Imperium won the war for Terra and lost the future it had been built to create.
</LoreQuote>

## The age that followed

The Emperor was placed upon the Golden Throne. The surviving loyalist Legions pursued the traitors during the Great Scouring, then divided into smaller Chapters under the Codex Astartes. Imperial institutions hardened around fear of another rebellion.

The Heresy is the setting's central wound. Nearly every major Imperial tradition, Chaos rivalry, and Space Marine inheritance carries its memory.
