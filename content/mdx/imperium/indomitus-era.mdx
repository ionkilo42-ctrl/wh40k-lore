## The galaxy breaks

The destruction of Cadia during Abaddon's Thirteenth Black Crusade destabilized the network of pylons that had constrained Warp influence around the Eye of Terror. The resulting Cicatrix Maledictum tore across the galaxy. Warp storms swallowed routes, systems vanished from contact, and daemonic invasions erupted.

The Imperium was divided. Terra and the Astronomican remained on one side. Across the Rift, Imperium Nihilus entered an age of darkness with unreliable travel and communication.

## The returned son

Roboute Guilliman was restored to life through the efforts of Belisarius Cawl and Yvraine. He reached Terra, met the Emperor, and assumed the role of Lord Commander. His return offered the Imperium strategic direction but also forced him to confront what ten thousand years had made of his father's realm.

Guilliman launched the Indomitus Crusade with newly revealed Primaris Space Marines, reorganized fleets, and vast armies. The crusade stabilized key regions, reinforced threatened worlds, and attempted to restore lines of authority.

<Callout title="A changed chronology">
The Era Indomitus is not a clean sequence of victories. Warp distortion, conflicting records, and retellings make its chronology deliberately difficult, reflecting a galaxy where even timekeeping is under strain.
</Callout>

## Wars without a final front

The current era contains no single war. Necron dynasties awaken around the Pariah Nexus. Hive Fleet Leviathan drives new tendrils into the galactic west. Chaos forces exploit the Rift. Ork empires gather. Imperial commanders fight to hold territory that may already be isolated.

The Lion's return adds another loyalist Primarch to the age, but the Imperium remains too vast and broken for any individual to control.

<LoreQuote>
Indomitus does not mean the darkness has been defeated. It means the Imperium has decided to advance into it.
</LoreQuote>

## The current condition

The Era Indomitus is defined by scale and uncertainty. Humanity has gained new weapons and returned heroes, yet its strategic position is catastrophic. The Imperium continues not because victory is near, but because surrender would make extinction immediate.
