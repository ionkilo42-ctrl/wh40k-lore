## The heart of a dying empire

Deep beneath the Imperial Palace on Terra, the Emperor of Mankind remains bound to the Golden Throne. The machine sustains the shattered remnants of his body after the final battle of the Horus Heresy. It is also far more than life support: it is part psychic amplifier, part seal, and part infrastructure for the Imperium's survival.

The Throne's true origins and full design are obscure even to those charged with maintaining it. Technologies from multiple ages and traditions appear to have been joined into a singular mechanism beyond present Imperial understanding.

<LoreQuote>
The Imperium endures because its god cannot rise, cannot die, and cannot be allowed to fail.
</LoreQuote>

## The broken Webway project

The Emperor intended to free humanity from dependence on Warp travel by gaining access to the Webway, the ancient network used by the Aeldari. During the Horus Heresy, Magnus the Red attempted to warn the Emperor of betrayal. His psychic intrusion shattered protective wards around the human Webway project.

Daemons flooded toward the breach. The conflict known as the War Within the Webway consumed Custodians, Sisters of Silence, Mechanicum forces, and irreplaceable resources. The Emperor was eventually forced to remain upon the Throne to hold the gateway closed.

<Callout title="Three functions">
The Golden Throne sustains the Emperor's body, helps contain the breached Webway portal beneath the Palace, and enables the psychic structures on which the Imperium depends.
</Callout>

## The Astronomican

The Astronomican is the psychic beacon by which Navigators orient Imperial vessels in the Warp. It is generated through the sacrifice and labor of trained psykers and directed by the Emperor's power. Its light defines the practical reach of Imperial navigation.

The opening of the Great Rift obscured that light across Imperium Nihilus, showing how quickly Imperial unity collapses when the beacon cannot be seen.

## Ten thousand years of failure

The Throne is deteriorating. Its systems demand continuous maintenance by orders that understand procedures more readily than principles. Replacement parts are rare or impossible to fabricate. Entire institutions exist to feed, guard, interpret, and repair it.

The Golden Throne therefore embodies the Imperium itself: magnificent, cruel, indispensable, decaying, and sustained by sacrifices so vast that they have become routine.
