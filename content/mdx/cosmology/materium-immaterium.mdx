## The divided cosmos

The galaxy exists across two inseparable realms. The **Materium** is the physical universe: stars, planets, bodies, machines, distance, and time. It is the realm in which armies march and voidships burn. The **Immaterium**, commonly called the Warp, is a parallel dimension of psychic energy. It does not obey ordinary geometry or causality. Emotion has weight there. Thought leaves a wake.

Every sentient species touches the Warp to some degree. Most minds create only a faint impression. Powerful psykers can perceive it directly and shape its energies, while species such as the Aeldari possess a deep and dangerous psychic presence.

<LoreQuote>
The Warp is not another place beyond the stars. It is the shadow cast by every soul beneath them.
</LoreQuote>

## Reflection and feedback

The relationship between the realms is not passive. Events in realspace feed emotion into the Warp; the Warp returns pressure, visions, corruption, and entities into realspace. A war may create so much terror and rage that the local Immaterium becomes violent. That violence can then produce storms, possession, or daemonic incursion, deepening the original disaster.

This feedback loop explains one of the setting's central tragedies: the suffering of the galaxy helps create the forces that cause further suffering.

<Callout title="Key distinction">
The Warp is not simply an afterlife, a hyperspace route, or a realm of evil. It is a psychic medium shaped by sentient experience. Chaos dominates much of it because the galaxy has spent millennia feeding it extremes.
</Callout>

## Crossing the impossible distance

Humanity's empire depends on Warp travel. A voidship enters the Immaterium through a controlled breach and uses its currents to cross distances that would take generations in realspace. The ship's **Gellar field** maintains a bubble of material reality around the vessel. Without it, the raw Warp and its inhabitants can reach the crew.

Navigation requires the Astronomican, a psychic beacon projected from Terra. Navigators use their mutant sight to perceive that light through the shifting tides. Even with these protections, travel remains uncertain. A voyage may arrive late, early, or not at all.

## Tears between worlds

Where the boundary weakens, the two realms bleed together. Warp storms can isolate systems for centuries. Stable passages such as the Eye of Terror create regions where physical law and psychic symbolism mingle. The greatest wound is the **Cicatrix Maledictum**, the Great Rift, which split the galaxy after the fall of Cadia.

Understanding this divided cosmos is the first key to the setting. Faster-than-light travel, psychic power, daemons, the Chaos Gods, and the Emperor's role on the Golden Throne all follow from it.
