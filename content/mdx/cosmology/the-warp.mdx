## An ocean without shores

The Warp is described through metaphor because physical language fails inside it. Sailors speak of currents, storms, tides, and reefs. Psykers describe color without light and voices without sound. Time folds. Distance answers symbolism rather than measurement.

The Immaterium is not empty. Psychic impressions can gather coherence, and powerful patterns can become self-sustaining entities. Some are momentary predators. Others are daemons bound to vast powers. They are not biological life forms; they are ideas and hungers wearing temporary shapes.

## Warp storms

Violence in the Warp can make travel impossible. During Old Night, vast storms isolated human worlds and helped collapse the civilizations of the Dark Age of Technology. The birth of Slaanesh briefly calmed those storms, allowing the Emperor to begin the Great Crusade.

The Great Rift later repeated that isolation on a galactic scale. On its far side, Imperium Nihilus struggles without reliable sight of the Astronomican.

<Callout title="Why it matters">
The Imperium is politically enormous but logistically fragile. Messages, reinforcements, food, and authority all depend on dangerous Warp routes. A storm can turn a neighboring system into a legend.
</Callout>

## The predators within

Daemons are fragments and expressions of the Warp's greater powers. Their forms often reflect the emotions and myths through which mortals understand them. In realspace, they require breaches, possession, ritual, or other anchors. Their bodies are temporary impositions on material law.

The strongest protection is not courage alone. Imperial vessels rely on Gellar fields; sanctified places use wards and ritual; trained psykers learn discipline. None is infallible.

<LoreQuote>
To enter the Warp is to sail through the accumulated nightmares of every species that ever dreamed.
</LoreQuote>

## The price of connection

The Warp is also the source of psychic communication, prescience, sorcery, and many miraculous technologies. This makes it impossible to reject completely. Humanity survives because it uses the same medium that threatens to consume it.
